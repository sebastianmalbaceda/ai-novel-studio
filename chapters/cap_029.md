# Capítulo 29 — "El Protocolo Completo"

El silencio que siguió fue el tipo de silencio que tiene peso. Literalmente. Podía sentirlo presionando contra mi pecho como si el aire mismo se hubiera vuelto más denso.

—Espera... ¿espera? — balbuceé, retrocediendo medio paso. Mi mochila golpeó la papelera del pasillo y la hizo oscilar peligrosamente. — ¿Watcher_0 te dijo directamente que el protocolo estaba completo? ¿Cuándo? ¿Cómo se comunica contigo?

Lyra ladeó la cabeza, ese gesto tan característico de ella cuando procesaba información.

—No mediante comunicación verbal. Fue más bien una... fluctuación en el campo bioeléctrico del edificio. Cuando mencionaste tu nombre durante el abrazo de ayer, algo cambió. Watcher_0 registró una anomalía en la frecuencia de tu señal y decidió que era momento de actualizar su estado.

*Mi señal. Mi maldita señal coherente que apparently toda la estructura alienígena debajo del instituto puede detectar.*

—Entonces... ¿tú sabías todo este tiempo? ¿Desde ayer por la noche?

—No tenía confirmación until tonight — respondió Lyra, sus dedos trazando círculos inconscientes en el aire. — El sistema necesita procesar las variables antes de definir protocolos. Y ahora que los tiene...

Se detuvo. Yo casi le rogaba que continuara, pero algo en su expresión me decía que ella misma estaba tratando de descifrar qué venía después.

—Lyra, ¿qué significa exactamente "protocolo completo"? ¿Watcher_0 está... terminando algo? ¿Iniciando algo?

Ella apretó los labios. Era la primera vez que la veía buscar palabras que no le venían naturalmente.

—El protocolo completo significa que el nodo de tránsito ha alcanzado un estado operativo estable. Que la frecuencia correcta ha sido identificada y validada. That the conditions for the next phase are met.

—¿Qué siguiente fase?

—No tengo la información completa — admitió, y había algo casi vulnerable en cómo pronunció esas palabras. — Mis archivos no incluyen ese detalle. Solo sé que Watcher_0 me encontró a mí porque yo encontré primero a alguien con la frecuencia adecuada.

Alguien. Yo.

Tomé aire profundamente y me apoyé contra la pared del pasillo, dejando que el frío del metal me anclara a la realidad.

—Entonces todo esto, todo lo que ha pasado desde que llegaste, fue porque Watcher_0 te necesitaba para encontrarme. Y ahora que te encontró, que me encontró, dice que el trabajo está hecho. ¿Eso es lo que estás telling me?

—El protocolo está completo en terms of detection and validation — corrigió Lyra. — Pero eso no significa que el nodo esté inactivo. Significa que ahora sabe exactamente qué hacer con la información que tiene.

—¿Y qué va a hacer?

Lyra me miró directamente a los ojos. Sus pupilas habían recuperado ese brillo violeta intenso, pero había algo diferente en ellas. Algo que no había visto antes.

—No lo sé, Haruto. Y eso me preocupa.

We stood there in the empty hallway, the fluorescent lights humming above us like they always did, but everything felt different now. The mundane sound of the school at night had transformed into the background noise of something that transcended normal human concerns.

My phone buzzed in my pocket. I checked it automatically.

*Kenji: Dude where are you? The YouTube live chat is going crazy. People are asking about the weird signals we picked up last week. Should I tell them about Lyra?*

I stared at the message for a long moment, feeling Lyra's eyes on my phone screen.

—Kenji knows — said Lyra, not as a question. — He suspects. You've told him things.

—He's my best friend. And he has that video. The one from the rooftop.

—Yes. The video.

I typed quickly:

*Me: NO. Don't tell them anything. We're handling something. Will explain later. Promise.*

The three dots appeared and disappeared. Kenji was probably frustrated, but he would wait. He always did when it mattered.

—Lyra — I said, putting the phone away. — ¿Por qué viniste a buscarme tonight? ¿Por qué revelar todo esto ahora, cuando podrías haber esperado?

She tilted her head, and for a moment I saw her processing the question, sorting through possible answers.

—Because I needed to see your reaction when you learned the truth. Because the protocol being complete changes nothing about the fact that I'm still here. That I chose to be here.

—¿Pero no tienes miedo? Si Watcher_0 está behind all of this, if the node is just using us somehow...

—I'm always analyzing potential threats — she replied. — But fear is an emotional response that slows cognitive processing. However...

She paused, and something shifted in her expression. Something softer.

—However, I find that I don't want to stop processing this situation. I don't want to disconnect from what we've established here. The frequency that Watcher_0 validated is the same frequency that makes me want to stay. So yes, I chose to come find you tonight. Not because of the protocol. Because of us.

*Us.*

The word hung in the air between us, heavier than the silence had been.

—That's surprisingly romantic for someone who doesn't understand sarcasm — I managed, trying to lighten the mood because that's what I do when things get too intense.

Lyra blinked. — That was sarcasm, wasn't it?

—Yep.

—I detected the sarcasm. I just don't understand why people use it when they're trying to communicate something important.

—Because sometimes we use indirect communication when direct communication feels too... exposing.

She nodded slowly, filing that information away with everything else she was learning about being human.

We were still standing in the hallway, and the night was getting deeper around us. I should have been worried about being caught after hours, about my mother finding out, about the practical consequences of all this. But right now, the only thing that mattered was that Lyra was here, being honest with me, choosing to stay even when she didn't have to.

—Okay — I said finally. — So we know that Watcher_0 has completed some kind of protocol. We know that my frequency was validated. We know you're choosing to stay anyway. What we don't know is what comes next.

—I have three hypotheses — said Lyra, and I almost smiled at her very specific way of processing. — First: Watcher_0 will remain dormant now that the initial phase is complete. Second: Watcher_0 will attempt to make direct contact with you to initiate the next phase of whatever it's planning. Third: The next phase has already begun and we're experiencing the early stages without recognizing them.

—Those are all terrible options.

—They're the only options available based on current data.

I ran my hand through my hair, thinking. The third option was the most unsettling. If the next phase had already begun, what did that mean? What was already happening that we hadn't noticed?

—What if it's none of those? — I asked. — What if Watcher_0 completed one protocol and now we're moving into something completely different?

Lyra considered this. — The probability of an unknown third scenario is significantly lower than the three main hypotheses, but not zero. Humans do have a tendency to create unpredictable outcomes.

—¿That's a compliment or an insult?

—It's an observation with neutral emotional value.

I laughed despite everything, and Lyra's expression shifted into something that almost resembled confusion at my reaction.

—We should go home — I said. — We're both here after hours and if the security guard finds us, it's going to be really awkward to explain why the exchange student and the guy she's definitely not secretly dating are wandering the halls at midnight.

Lyra opened her mouth, then closed it.

—We are not secretly dating — she said carefully.

—Right. That's exactly what I said. Which is why it would be awkward if someone assumed otherwise.

—I understand the social logic, but I want to clarify that the lack of dating designation is not due to lack of trying on your part.

I choked on absolutely nothing.

—Did you just... was that a joke?

Lyra's expression remained perfectly neutral. — I learned that humans use humor to defuse tension. Am I doing it correctly?

—You're doing it terrifyingly correctly.

We started walking toward the exit, the hallway lights flickering slightly as we passed. Or maybe I was imagining that. It was hard to tell what was real anymore and what was just my brain trying to process too much information.

At the main doors, Lyra stopped.

—Haruto, I need to tell you something else.

I waited.

—The protocol being complete also means that I have access to certain data I didn't have before. Information that Watcher_0 has been accumulating for... for a very long time. About this location, about this school, about... about your mother.

My blood went cold.

—What about my mother?

Lyba looked at me with those violet eyes that saw everything, understood everything, and was starting to know things that I wasn't ready to hear.

—There's a reason she teaches here. A reason she transferred to this school seventeen years ago. Exactly nine months before you were born.

I couldn't breathe.

—She knew — I said, the words coming out as barely a whisper. — She knew about the node. About Watcher_0. About... about me.
